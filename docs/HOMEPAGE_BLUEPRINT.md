# Homepage Blueprint

## Homepage Goal

The homepage should immediately position Colorado Snomo Expo as the Rocky Mountain region's premier winter powersports expo and move visitors toward the next practical action.

Primary goals:

- Establish the event as premium, regional, seasonal, and worth planning around.
- Help attendees understand what they can do at the expo: explore new machines, meet exhibitors, browse gear, attend the swap meet, view the floorplan, and plan their visit.
- Help exhibitors and sponsors quickly find the path to participate.
- Make ticket expectations clear: tickets are sold at the box office only.

The homepage should feel clean, premium, cinematic, and uncluttered. It should avoid sounding like a generic trade show, a discount event, or a busy community bulletin board.

## Hero Layout

Approved hero copy:

- Event name: `Colorado Snomo Expo`
- Headline: `Your Winter Starts Here.`
- Supporting line: `The Rocky Mountain Region's Premier Winter Powersports Expo.`

Hero direction:

- Use a full-bleed cinematic winter powersports image or video-style still as the emotional anchor.
- The first viewport should clearly communicate snow, speed, machines, Colorado, and winter anticipation.
- Keep text over the image, not inside a card.
- Use strong contrast so the headline is readable without making the page feel dark or muddy.
- Keep the composition spacious. The hero should feel confident, not crowded.
- Show a hint of the next section below the fold on desktop and mobile.

Hero content order:

1. `Colorado Snomo Expo`
2. `Your Winter Starts Here.`
3. `The Rocky Mountain Region's Premier Winter Powersports Expo.`
4. Event date, venue, and city when finalized.
5. Primary CTA group.

Hero CTA group:

- Primary: `Plan Your Visit`
- Secondary: `View Exhibitors`
- Utility link: `Tickets at Box Office`

Ticket language:

- Use: `Tickets available at the box office only.`
- Use: `Get tickets at the box office.`
- Avoid online ticket-purchase language or any checkout implication.

## Navigation

Navigation should be simple, stable, and event-focused.

Recommended desktop navigation:

- `Exhibitors`
- `Schedule`
- `Floorplan`
- `Swap Meet`
- `Plan Your Visit`
- `Sponsor`

Recommended mobile navigation:

- Collapsed menu with the same items.
- Persistent top-level access to `Plan Your Visit`.
- Do not overload the mobile header with more than one visible CTA.

Navigation behavior:

- Header may begin transparent over the hero and transition to a clean solid background after scroll.
- Keep logo/event name readable at all times.
- Use short labels. Avoid clever menu names.
- Do not include online ticket-purchase language.

## CTA Hierarchy

Primary CTA:

- `Plan Your Visit`
- Purpose: Move attendees to practical event details.

Secondary CTAs:

- `View Exhibitors`
- `See Schedule`
- `Explore the Floorplan`

Audience-specific CTAs:

- Attendees: `Plan Your Visit`
- Exhibitors: `Become an Exhibitor`
- Sponsors: `Sponsor the Expo`
- Swap meet sellers: `Swap Meet Details`

Ticket CTA rule:

- Ticket-related CTAs must communicate box office availability only.
- Approved label examples: `Tickets at Box Office`, `Box Office Ticket Info`, `Admission Details`.
- Do not use online ticket-purchase language.

## Countdown Concept

The countdown should create seasonal anticipation without turning the page into a loud promo timer.

Placement:

- Directly below the hero or integrated into the lower hero band.
- Keep it compact and elegant.

Content:

- Days, hours, minutes, and seconds until doors open.
- Event date and opening time.
- Short supporting line: `The season starts at the expo.`

Design:

- Use restrained typography and clear numeric blocks.
- Avoid flashing, pulsing, or urgent sale-style behavior.
- Do not imply ticket scarcity unless that is confirmed.

Fallback states:

- Before date is finalized: show `Event dates coming soon.`
- During event weekend: show `Expo weekend is here.`
- After event: replace countdown with post-event seasonal messaging and next-year interest capture.

## Dual Audience Section

The homepage should serve both attendees and industry participants without making either path feel secondary.

Section title:

- `Built for Riders. Made for the Industry.`

Audience column: Attendees

- Explore new snowmobiles, gear, trailers, accessories, and winter services.
- Meet dealers, manufacturers, clubs, and local experts.
- Browse the swap meet and plan the season ahead.
- CTA: `Plan Your Visit`

Audience column: Exhibitors and Sponsors

- Connect with Rocky Mountain riders before the season hits.
- Showcase products, services, inventory, and brand presence.
- Reach a highly targeted winter powersports audience.
- CTA: `Become an Exhibitor`
- Secondary CTA: `Sponsor the Expo`

Design:

- Use two balanced panels or a split full-width band.
- Avoid making this section look like a pricing table.
- Keep copy concise and practical.

## Why Attend Section

Purpose:

- Give visitors quick, scannable reasons to attend.
- Reinforce that the event is useful, exciting, and season-starting.

Suggested title:

- `Everything You Need Before the Snow Flies`

Content pillars:

- `See What's New`  
  New machines, gear, accessories, trailers, technology, and winter services.

- `Meet the People Behind the Brands`  
  Dealers, exhibitors, clubs, and regional experts in one place.

- `Gear Up for the Season`  
  Compare options, discover deals, and find what belongs in the trailer.

- `Connect with the Community`  
  Riders, families, clubs, and businesses gathering before winter begins.

Design:

- Use four concise feature blocks with strong iconography or imagery.
- Keep each block short. No paragraph-heavy cards.
- Use real powersports imagery where available.

## Featured Exhibitors

Purpose:

- Show credibility and give exhibitors visible value.
- Help attendees understand the range of brands and vendors.

Content:

- Featured exhibitor logos.
- Short category labels where useful: dealers, manufacturers, gear, trailers, clubs, services.
- Optional featured exhibitor spotlight with one image, name, category, and brief line.

CTA:

- Primary: `View All Exhibitors`
- Secondary: `Become an Exhibitor`

Design:

- Use a clean logo grid on a white or near-white section.
- Keep logo sizes consistent.
- Avoid overly decorative frames.
- Do not make sponsor logos compete with exhibitor content unless the section is explicitly sponsored.

Empty or early-season state:

- If exhibitors are not finalized, show `Exhibitor announcements coming soon.`
- Include `Interested in exhibiting?` with a CTA to `Become an Exhibitor`.

## Floorplan Preview

Purpose:

- Give visitors confidence that the event is organized and easy to navigate.
- Tease booth discovery without requiring the full floorplan experience on the homepage.

Content:

- Preview image or simplified interactive map of the expo floor.
- Highlight major areas: exhibitors, swap meet, food, registration or box office, entrances, restrooms, and featured zones.
- Include a few callouts only.

CTA:

- `Explore the Floorplan`

Design:

- Keep the preview legible at a glance.
- Use labels sparingly.
- Avoid tiny booth numbers on the homepage preview.
- The full floorplan can hold the detailed booth list.

Fallback state:

- If the floorplan is not final, show a polished placeholder: `Floorplan coming soon.`

## Schedule Preview

Purpose:

- Give visitors a quick reason to stay longer and plan their day.

Content:

- Show the next three to five featured schedule items.
- Include day, time, title, and location if available.
- Prioritize high-interest items: doors open, featured demos, clinics, meetups, presentations, swap meet hours, and special appearances.

CTA:

- `See Full Schedule`

Design:

- Use a simple timeline or agenda list.
- Keep time information prominent.
- Avoid dense calendar grids on the homepage.

Fallback state:

- If schedule details are not finalized, show `Schedule announcements coming soon.`

## Swap Meet

Purpose:

- Make the swap meet visible as a distinct attendee draw and seller opportunity.

Suggested title:

- `Swap Meet`

Content:

- Briefly explain what visitors can expect: used parts, gear, accessories, equipment, and hard-to-find winter powersports items.
- Clarify seller participation details only when confirmed.
- Link to rules, hours, registration, or booth details when available.

CTA:

- Attendee CTA: `Browse Swap Meet Details`
- Seller CTA: `Swap Meet Seller Info`

Design:

- Use authentic, practical imagery.
- Keep it energetic but not cluttered.
- Do not make the swap meet feel like a flea market disconnected from the premium expo experience.

## Plan Your Visit

Purpose:

- Consolidate practical event details so visitors know how to attend.

Content:

- Dates and hours.
- Venue name and address.
- Parking guidance.
- Admission details.
- Box office ticket note.
- Accessibility information.
- Nearby lodging or travel resources if relevant.

Approved ticket copy:

- `Tickets are available at the box office only.`

CTA:

- `Get Directions`
- `Admission Details`

Design:

- Use a clean information layout, not a dense FAQ wall.
- Include a map preview or venue image if available.
- Keep the ticket note visible but calm.

## Sponsors

Purpose:

- Recognize sponsor support and provide a path for sponsorship inquiries.

Content:

- Sponsor logo rows by tier if tiers exist.
- Short sponsor invitation copy.
- CTA: `Sponsor the Expo`

Design:

- Keep logos balanced and readable.
- Use tier labels only if they are confirmed.
- Avoid giving sponsors more visual weight than the core event story unless contractually required.

Empty or early-season state:

- `Sponsor announcements coming soon.`
- CTA remains: `Sponsor the Expo`

## Footer

Footer should be compact, useful, and complete.

Recommended footer content:

- Event name: `Colorado Snomo Expo`
- Short line: `Your Winter Starts Here.`
- Event date and venue when finalized.
- Navigation links: Exhibitors, Schedule, Floorplan, Swap Meet, Plan Your Visit, Sponsor.
- Contact link or email.
- Social links.
- Legal or policy links if needed.

Ticket reminder:

- Include a small note: `Tickets available at the box office only.`

Avoid:

- Large newsletter block unless there is a confirmed email strategy.
- Repeating every homepage CTA.
- Overly dense link lists.

## Seasonal Homepage Behavior

The homepage should change state as the event cycle changes.

Pre-announcement:

- Focus on brand, season energy, and interest capture.
- Use `Dates coming soon` if dates are not confirmed.

Announcement phase:

- Lead with date, venue, and exhibitor or sponsor calls to action.
- Countdown may begin once date and opening time are confirmed.

Pre-event phase:

- Prioritize Plan Your Visit, exhibitors, schedule, floorplan, and swap meet.
- Make box office ticket language clear.

Event weekend:

- Replace the countdown with `Expo weekend is here.`
- Prioritize hours, directions, parking, admission details, and schedule.

Post-event:

- Thank attendees, exhibitors, and sponsors.
- Show highlights if available.
- Invite exhibitors and sponsors to inquire for next year.
- Use `Next year's details coming soon` until confirmed.

## Mobile Behavior

Mobile should feel fast, focused, and easy to act on.

Rules:

- Hero copy must remain readable without covering the essential subject of the image.
- CTAs should stack cleanly.
- The primary visible action should be `Plan Your Visit`.
- Navigation should collapse into a simple menu.
- Countdown should become a compact two-row or swipe-free layout.
- Dual audience content should stack: attendees first, exhibitors and sponsors second.
- Feature sections should become single-column.
- Floorplan preview should link to the full view rather than forcing complex interaction on the homepage.
- Tap targets should be generous and predictable.

Avoid:

- Tiny booth labels on mobile.
- Large paragraphs inside cards.
- Sticky elements that consume too much vertical space.
- Multiple competing CTAs in the mobile header.

## Animation Rules

Animation should support a premium cinematic feeling without slowing down the page or distracting from event details.

Allowed:

- Subtle hero image motion or video-like background treatment.
- Gentle fade and rise transitions as sections enter view.
- Smooth header background transition on scroll.
- Calm countdown number updates.
- Light hover states for cards, links, logos, and buttons.

Rules:

- Keep motion slow, restrained, and purposeful.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Do not animate important text in a way that delays readability.
- Do not use flashing, bouncing, shaking, confetti, or sale-style urgency.
- Avoid heavy animation on mobile and low-power devices.

## Things to Avoid

Do not use:

- Online ticket-purchase language
- Online checkout language unless an online ticketing flow actually exists.
- Cluttered hero layouts.
- Generic stock business imagery.
- Overly busy snowflake graphics or novelty winter decoration.
- Dense blocks of copy.
- Too many competing CTAs above the fold.
- Discount-first messaging.
- Loud countdown urgency.
- Overly dark, muddy, or hard-to-read imagery.
- A landing page that only markets the event without helping visitors act.
- Sponsor or exhibitor grids that feel randomly sized or visually messy.
- Mobile layouts that require pinching, zooming, or horizontal scrolling.

The homepage should make one thing feel obvious: winter starts here, and Colorado Snomo Expo is where riders, brands, and the regional powersports community gather to get ready.
